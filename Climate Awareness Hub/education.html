Climate Education

Learn about causes, effects and what communities in Sierra Leone can do to adapt and mitigate risks.

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns, primarily caused by human activities increasing greenhouse gases.

Causes

  • 🏭 Fossil fuel emissions — Burning coal, oil and gas releases CO₂ and other greenhouse gases. Reducing energy use and switching to clean energy lowers emissions.
  • 🌲 Deforestation — Clearing forests reduces carbon storage and increases erosion. Protecting and restoring forests helps climate and livelihoods.
  • 🚜 Agricultural practices — Some farming and livestock produce methane and nitrous oxide; sustainable farming reduces impacts while improving yields.

Effects

Rising temperatures increase extreme weather, sea-level rise, and changing rainfall patterns. This affects water supplies, food security, health, and infrastructure.

Climate challenges in Sierra Leone

Sierra Leone faces heightened flood risk in coastal and riverine communities, erosion, and pressure on agriculture. Urban drainage and informal settlements are particularly vulnerable.

Flood awareness & tips

  • 🛟 Know evacuation routes — Learn local shelters and safe high ground.
  • 🚰 Store clean water — Prepare safe water and purification options.
  • 🧯 Secure valuables & documents — Keep copies in a waterproof bag.

Further learning & sources

For reliable information, consult IPCC reports, national meteorological services, and local NGOs. Link these sources and keep materials up to date.
